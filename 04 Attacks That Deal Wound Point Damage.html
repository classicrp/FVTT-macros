Some attacks can be used to deal wound points damage directly.

Critical Hits: Only the dice are rolled for Critical Hits, all other bonuses only count with the initial roll. For example, if your weapon has a x3 or higher multiplier, then the roll would be multiple dice (i.e. a Greataxe has a x3 critical multiplier, so the initial damage roll would be 1d12 plus all bonuses, the extra damage from the critical would be 2d12 only). When a creature is subject to a critical hit, the critical hit deals the damage normally, reducing vigor points first, and then reducing wound points when vigor points are gone. It also directly deals an amount of wound point damage equal to its critical multiplier (for example, 3 wound points for a weapon with a Ã—3 modifier), on top of any wound point damage the creature might take from the critical hit.

Negative Energy Damage: When a creature deals negative energy damage to a creature with a spell or effect, it can choose to deal wound point or vigor point damage (but not both) with the spell or effect. If that creature chooses to deal vigor point damage, The spell or effect deals negative energy damage normally, and that damage reduces vigor points only, even if it deals more damage than the target has vigor points. and then reducing wound points when vigor points are gone. Upon a Critical Hit (with an attack) or a Critical Failure Spell Save (for any effect with a DC), if the spell or effect deals negative energy damage to wound points directly, it deals an amount of wound point damage equal to the number of dice the creature would roll for that effect; if the effect deals a number of points per caster level (such as the harm spell), it deals a number of wound points equal to the caster level of the spell. If it is from a melee attack (such as a Spectre's touch), it instead deals an additional 2 wound points.
