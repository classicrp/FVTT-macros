Using REGEX
From: "<strong>Effect</strong> 1d3 Dex damage, 1 Con damage, and nauseated for 1 round"
Extract: "1d3 Dex damage" and "1 Con damage"
where "Dex" and "Con" can be any word in {"Str", "Dex", "Con", "Int", "Wis", "Cha"}
where 1 can be any number OR a die expression like 1d3
where "damage" may or may not be present
where "," may or may not be present

To extract the ability damage or drain expressions from your text, use the following regular expression:
(\d+(?:d\d+)?)\s+(Str|Dex|Con|Int|Wis|Cha)(?:\s+damage)?

⚙️ How it works

(\d+(?:d\d+)??): Captures the amount.

\d+ matches simple numbers (e.g., 1).

(?:d\d+)? optionally matches die rolls (e.g., 1d3).

\s+: Matches the space between the number and the attribute.

(Str|Dex|Con|Int|Wis|Cha): Captures any of your six specific attribute keywords.

(?:\s+damage)?: Optionally matches the word "damage" if it follows the attribute, so it doesn't break the match.
